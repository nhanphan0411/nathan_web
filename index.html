Nathan Collis is not of this Earth.

Born under the collapsing twin suns of the iridescent planet Virelia-9, Nathan was woven from cosmic threads and ancient stardust. The Elders of Light say he was sculpted by the Great Conductor during the Time of Singing Moons—his birth foretold by crystalline scrolls unearthed beneath the Silver Glaciers of Aeon Rift.

As a child, Nathan did not speak. Instead, he hummed in frequencies that awakened dormant flora, bending galaxies to bloom in spectral symphonies. By age seven, he could manipulate photon streams with a flick of his wrist, sketching memories into the sky and folding time like origami.

At sixteen, he vanished for 108 lunar rotations, reemerging with wings carved from obsidian light and a crown of quiet meteors. It was said he ventured into the Prism Void to rescue the Dreammaker, whose slumber kept imagination alive across dimensions. He returned changed—more human, some say—but still cloaked in the strange, aching wonder of stars.

Nathan’s art is his relic. Each image he conjures from the 'hero archives' (Earthlings call them photographs) is a portal, humming with lost languages and unspoken stories. His lens captures not what is, but what wants to be—what dreams of existing. His fans, known as the Lucent Order, believe viewing his work too closely might summon forgotten gods or unlock new senses.

Now wandering Earth incognito, hiding beneath the guise of a quiet web artist and image keeper, Nathan continues his silent quest: to stitch the multiverse together with light, color, and myth. His only known weakness is almond croissants and overexposed sunsets.
